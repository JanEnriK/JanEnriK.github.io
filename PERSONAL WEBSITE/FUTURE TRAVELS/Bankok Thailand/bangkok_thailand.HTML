     Bangkok, Thai Krung Thep is the city, capital, and chief port of Thailand. It is the only cosmopolitan city in a country of small towns and villages and is Thailand’s cultural and commercial centre. The city’s maze of canals connected with the river earned it the name “Venice of the East.” Most of the city’s population are ethnic Thais. The Chinese are by far the largest minority, but there are sizable communities of other Asians, North Americans, and Europeans.

      Bangkok Chinatown is the largest Chinatown in the world. It is also, quite arguably, one of the best Chinatowns to visit as well. While many countries have Chinatowns, none compare to Bangkok’s; here you’ll discover what makes it so unique. When it comes to Thai and Chinese food Chinatown is the place to be in Bangkok.
